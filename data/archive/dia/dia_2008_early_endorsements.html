Endorsements by Congressmen, Senators and Governors--Feb. 5 Edition
last revised Feb. 27, 2008  alpha by state  See also Early Endorsements.  Timeline.
This list shows leading officials who endorsed before the February 5 contests1 based on releases from the campaigns and review of news accounts2.  Several candidates dropped out in January (Giuliani, Hunter and FThompson on the Republican side and Biden, Dodd, Edwards, Kucinich, and Richardson on the Democratic side) and some of their backers endorsed remaining candidates.

Governors endorsing: 27 - Huckabee (1), McCain (7), Romney (3); Clinton (10+AS), Obama (6).
U.S. Senators endorsing: 41 - Huckabee (0), McCain (15), Romney (7); Clinton (11), Obama (8) ...and 3 running.
U.S. House members endorsing: 201 - Huckabee (5), McCain (28), Romney (39); Clinton (76+USVI), Obama (53+AS) ...and 1 running.
These numbers do not include officials who endorsed candidates who have withdrawn and who have not made an second endorsement.

Notes.
1. For the Republicans, the most noteworthy change since the beginning of January was a number of key endorsements McCain picked up shortly before primaries (for example Gov. Crist and Sen. Martinez in Florida; Gov. Schwarzenegger in California and Sens. Isakson and Chambliss in Georgia and Coburn in Oklahoma).  For the Democrats, Obama showed considerable momentum, picking up some big endorsements (Govs. Napolitano, Sebelius and Doyle, Sens. Kerry, Kennedy and others) as well as a passel of U.S. House members).
2. There may be a few omissions; the Obama campaign in particular did not generally trumpet its endorsements.
